The NOT Operator (¬)

The NOT operator, or logical negation, is a unary operation that flips the truth value of its operand. It is the simplest yet most vital logical tool for defining contradictions.

In digital systems, NOT gates (inverters) are used to flip bits, turning 0 into 1 and vice versa, which is fundamental for binary arithmetic.
